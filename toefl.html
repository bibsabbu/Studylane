TOEFL- Test Of English as per Foreign Langauge

The TOEFL test evaluates the four key language skills: Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing. The scoring procedure for TOEFL is somewhat similar to that of PTE Academic, although the test format and specific scoring criteria may vary.

Scoring Procedure

Here's a brief overview of the scoring procedure for TOEFL:

  1. Reading Section

    2. The Reading section consists of multiple-choice questions based on passages. Each correct answer earns points, and the total score is reported on a scale of 0 to 30.

  2. Listening Section

    3. The Listening section includes listening comprehension tasks, such as answering questions based on audio recordings. Similar to the Reading section, the total score for the Listening section is reported on a scale of 0 to 30.

  3. Speaking Section

    4. In the Speaking section, test-takers respond to speaking prompts, and their responses are evaluated based on various linguistic features, including pronunciation, fluency, vocabulary, grammar, and coherence. Speaking scores range from 0 to 30.

  4. Writing Section

    5. The Writing section requires test-takers to write essays based on given prompts. Responses are assessed based on content, organization, language use, and grammar. Writing scores also range from 0 to 30.

  5. Overall Score

    6. The overall score for the TOEFL test is the sum of the scores obtained in each section. The total score ranges from 0 to 120.

  6. Score Report

    7. Test-takers receive score reports approximately 10 days after the test date. Score reports include the overall score, as well as scores for each individual section of the test.

  7. Score Validity

    8. TOEFL scores are valid for up to two years from the date of the test.

TOEFL scores are widely accepted by universities, colleges, and organizations around the world as evidence of English language proficiency for academic and professional purposes.
