Advantages of Studying in New Zealand
  • High-Quality Education:

    • New Zealand is renowned for its high-quality education system, with universities, colleges, and institutes offering a wide range of programs and degrees. The country's institutions maintain rigorous academic standards and are globally recognized for their excellence.

  • Cultural Experience:

    • Studying in New Zealand offers students a rich cultural experience, with opportunities to engage with the country's indigenous Maori culture, festivals, traditions, and arts. Students can also interact with people from diverse cultural backgrounds and develop global perspectives.

  • Safe and Peaceful Environment:

    • New Zealand is consistently ranked as one of the safest and most peaceful countries in the world. It offers a welcoming and inclusive environment for international students, with a diverse and multicultural society.

  • Work Oppertunities:

    • International students studying in New Zealand are allowed to work part-time during the academic year and full-time during scheduled breaks. This provides students with opportunities to gain valuable work experience, earn income, and support themselves financially while studying.

  • Research and Innovations:

    • New Zealand universities are actively engaged in research and innovation across various fields, including agriculture, environmental science, marine biology, and more. Students can participate in cutting-edge research projects and benefit from state-of-the-art facilities and resources.

  • Quality Of Life:

    • New Zealand offers a high quality of life, with clean and green cities, a well-developed healthcare system, and a balanced lifestyle. The country's relatively small population contributes to a sense of community and safety.

Post-Study Oppertunities
  • Post-Study Work Visa (PSWV):

    • International students who have completed a qualification in New Zealand may be eligible to apply for a Post-Study Work Visa (Open). This visa allows graduates to work in any job in New Zealand for up to 12 months, regardless of the relevance of the job to their field of study.

    Graduates who have a job offer relevant to their qualification may be eligible to apply for a Post-Study Work Visa (Employer Assisted). This visa allows graduates to work in New Zealand for up to two or three years, depending on the location of their job offer and the skill level of the job.

  • Pathways to Permanent Residency:

    • New Zealand offers various pathways for international students to obtain permanent residency, including the Skilled Migrant Category, Essential Skills Work Visa, and Entrepreneur Work Visa. Graduates with qualifications and skills needed in New Zealand may qualify for these immigration pathways.

  • Access to Healthcare

    • International students who reside in New Zealand may be eligible for healthcare services under the country's public healthcare system. They may also choose to purchase private health insurance for additional coverage and benefits.

  • Sattlement Support Service:

    • New Zealand provides settlement support services to help newcomers adjust to life in the country. These services may include language classes, orientation sessions, employment workshops, and assistance with housing, healthcare, and other essential needs.

  • Networking and Professional Development:

    • International students have the opportunity to network with professionals in their field of study through industry events, job fairs, and professional associations. Engaging in networking activities can help them explore career opportunities and build professional connections in New Zealand.

  • Access to Social Services and Benefits:

    • International students who reside in New Zealand may be eligible for certain social services and benefits, such as childcare subsidies, public transportation discounts, and tax credits. Eligibility for these services may vary depending on individual circumstances and immigration status.
